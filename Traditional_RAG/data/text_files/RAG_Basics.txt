RAG Basics

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) is an advanced AI technique that combines information retrieval with 
text generation. It enhances the capabilities of large language models by allowing them to access external 
knowledge sources before generating responses. Instead of relying only on pre-trained data, RAG systems search 
databases, documents, or websites to retrieve relevant information and produce more accurate and up-to-date answers.

RAG works in two main stages: retrieval and generation. In the retrieval stage, the system searches for relevant 
data from a knowledge base using similarity search or embeddings. In the generation stage, the retrieved information is 
passed to a language model, which generates a meaningful and context-aware response. Technologies such as LangChain and vector 
databases like Pinecone are commonly used to build RAG-based applications.

Types of RAG Systems
 - Naive RAG
 - Advanced RAG
 - Modular RAG
 - Hybrid RAG
 - Graph-based RAG

Applications of RAG

RAG is widely used in modern AI applications where accurate and updated information is important. It is commonly 
applied in chatbots, virtual assistants, customer support systems, and enterprise search tools. In healthcare and finance, 
RAG helps provide reliable answers from large document collections, while in education it supports intelligent tutoring systems. 
By combining retrieval and generation, RAG improves response quality, reduces hallucinations, and enables AI systems to deliver more 
trustworthy information.
